# Idea

## One-line product

Unfading is a private iOS memory map for recurring groups that lets friends leave stories in shared places through pins, photos, notes, reactions, and rewind reminders.

## Problem

People already capture moments, but they do not preserve the place-based emotional context around those moments. Chat albums, photo feeds, and map reviews fail at one critical question: "What happened here with us?" Users should not have to search years of chat logs just to remember a dinner, a trip stop, or a birthday location.

## Target user

- Primary user: a recurring group organizer in their 20s or 30s who manages meetups, dinners, or trips.
- Secondary user: emotionally motivated friend groups who want a shared travel or social diary.
- Group assumption: the product should support 3+ person groups well. Couple-only positioning is not enough.

## Why now

- Memory logging, photo journaling, and location-based behavior are already mainstream, but the market still lacks a strong group-centered spatial memory product.
- Existing tools are optimized for public sharing, place discovery, or one-to-one intimacy, not for preserving group memory over time.
- Native iOS features such as MapKit, location permissions, notifications, and polished interaction patterns make this category especially strong as a native product.

## Market gap

- Instagram / Band-style feeds: good for posting, weak for place-specific recall.
- Map review apps: good for discovery, weak for memory preservation.
- Couple diary apps: good for 2-person intimacy, weak for friend groups.
- General diary apps: good for private journaling, weak for shared history.

## Product vision

"Leave our stories in the places we shared."

The product should turn repeated visits, photos, short notes, and emotion tags into a visible shared archive. Over time, the group should feel like it has built a living memory map, not just a pile of images.

## Success condition

- A group can create its own memory space and invite members without friction.
- Shared places become the main organizing unit for memories.
- Users can rediscover old moments through map exploration and rewind reminders.
- The product feels intimate, native, and safe for App Store review.
