## Challenge Section

### Objection (self-recorded)
My earlier iterations (v5.2, v5.3) followed REG §11 correctly. By v5.4, I started treating "implementation" commits as exempt from the meeting requirement. That was wrong — REG §11 says amendments, not "only doc-only amendments". The moment I changed REGULATION or bumped versions, a meeting was required. I accepted Codex's R7 critique as "peer review" but didn't treat that critique as triggering the meeting-creation obligation. Classic procedural drift.

### Risk
The `this-entry` self-exemption rule I added could be abused by someone marking every entry as meta to skip meetings. Mitigation: this meeting file exists specifically so v5.6 doesn't use its own exemption; future meta-amendments should follow the same pattern.

### Rejected alternative
Removing REG §11 entirely because it was being violated. Rejected — the rule is correct, my compliance was broken. Fix the compliance, not the rule.

### Uncertainty deferred
Checker currently only validates file EXISTENCE, not meeting content. Honest-agent scope accepts this — content validation is subjective and can't be mechanized reliably.
